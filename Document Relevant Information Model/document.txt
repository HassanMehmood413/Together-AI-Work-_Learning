The giant panda (Ailuropoda melanoleuca), also known as the panda bear or simply panda, is a bear species endemic to China. It is easily recognized by its black and white coloring and is known for its bamboo diet. Conservation efforts have helped increase its population in the wild.
The llama is a domesticated South American camelid, widely used as a meat and pack animal by Andean cultures since the pre-Columbian era. Llamas are known for their gentle temperament and social behavior, making them excellent companions for humans.
The wild Bactrian camel (Camelus ferus) is an endangered species of camel endemic to Northwest China and southwestern Mongolia. This unique camel is adapted to life in the harsh deserts of Central Asia, characterized by its ability to survive without water for long periods.
The guanaco is a camelid native to South America, closely related to the llama. Guanacos are one of two wild South American camelids; the other species is the vicuña, which lives at higher elevations. They are known for their agility and ability to thrive in the diverse climates of the Andes.
The vicuña is a wild South American camelid that lives in the high alpine areas of the Andes. It is renowned for its fine wool, which is highly prized. Vicuñas are known for their elegant form and are typically seen in small herds.
